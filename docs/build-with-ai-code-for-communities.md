## Overview

Build with AI: Code for Communities is a national hackathon by Google Cloud that connects developers, designers, and AI builders directly with sitting Members of Parliament to solve real, on-the-ground governance problems in constituency development planning, environment, healthcare, and agriculture.

Every problem statement was submitted by an MP's office, describing a gap they are actively trying to close for their constituents right now. The best solutions won't just win a prize pool — they'll be piloted in a PHC, a constituency, or a district, with the possibility to scale if the pilot works.

The top teams will travel to an **in-person demo day in New Delhi**, where they present in front of the MPs and other industry experts.

The hackathon asks builders to close the governance gap using AI as the actual engine that turns scattered, unstructured citizen input and fragmented public data into something an MP, a district health officer, or a farmer can act on today.

## Challenges (Problem Statements)

Four problem statements are open for this edition:

| Track | Theme | Title |
|---|---|---|
| **Track 01** | Constituency | People's Priorities |
| **Track 02** | Environment | CleanAir & Clear Streets |
| **Track 03** | Healthcare | Smart Health |
| **Track 04** | Agriculture | Kisan Alert |

### Track 1 — People's Priorities (Constituency / AI for Constituency Development Planning)

**The Problem:** MPs receive development requests through public meetings, letters, social media, grievance portals, and direct representations — while local development plans contain dozens of competing proposed projects. There's no objective way to consolidate citizen feedback, spot recurring needs, and weigh competing proposals against real demand (for example, comparing requests for school upgrades against enrollment and travel-distance data versus a proposed vocational centre).

**The Challenge:** Build a multilingual AI platform where citizens can submit development suggestions via voice, text, photos, or messaging apps. The system should analyze submissions to surface recurring themes, map demand hotspots, and combine citizen feedback with demographic data, infrastructure gaps, local development plans, and public datasets — to recommend and rank high-priority development works an MP can act on.

### Track 2 — CleanAir & Clear Streets (Environment / Spotting and Fixing Local Pollution Hotspots)

**The Problem:** City-level air quality apps miss hyper-local events — a garbage dump fire, an industrial cluster, a smog trap at a busy junction — because local authorities can't have eyes on every street. These pockets go unnoticed while directly harming nearby residents.

**The Challenge:** Build a neighbourhood-level pollution map combining citizen-uploaded photos of smoke/dust, local sensor readings, and satellite imagery. The system should automatically detect hidden pollution hotspots, predict air quality spikes over the next 24 hours, and alert municipal teams so they can deploy resources (water-mist cannons, cleanup crews) exactly where needed.

### Track 3 — Smart Health (Healthcare / AI-Driven Health Center & Supply Chain Management)

**The Problem:** PHCs and CHCs face recurring operational gaps — medicine stock-outs, unmanaged patient footfall, bed unavailability, unpredictable doctor attendance — tracked manually with no real-time visibility, leading to shortages and under-resourced facilities.

**The Challenge:** Build a multilingual AI platform for real-time health centre management covering stock monitoring, patient footfall, bed availability, doctor attendance, and test availability audits. It should generate early stock-out warnings, AI-driven demand forecasts, and smart resource redistribution recommendations across a district's PHCs/CHCs — and automatically flag underperforming or under-resourced centres to district administrators for intervention.

### Track 4 — Kisan Alert (Agriculture / Smart Water, Crop & Advisory System)

**The Problem:** Farmers often face crop failure from unpredictable monsoons and a lack of data-driven guidance — crop choices are based on habit or hearsay rather than soil health, groundwater depth, or rainfall data, leading to financial loss and wasted resources.

**The Challenge:** Build a voice-and-SMS agricultural intelligence platform in Indic languages for small and marginal farmers, with three components:

- A smart crop recommendation engine using satellite and soil data.
- Real-time advisory and dry-spell alerts using localized weather forecasts and ground sensor data for irrigation/fertilization guidance.
- Crop health logging via photo/voice for AI diagnosis, connected directly to Rythu Seva Kendras for expert follow-up.

### Tools & Tech You Can Use

You're free to build with any stack, but the following are recommended and supported with documentation/mentor familiarity (and credits, where available):

| Category | Recommended Tools |
|---|---|
| **AI/ML & Generative AI** | Gemini API, Vertex AI (model building, fine-tuning, agents), Google AI Studio |
| **Language & Voice** | Cloud Speech-to-Text & Text-to-Speech, Dialogflow (voice/SMS conversational flows), Translation API |
| **Vision** | Vertex AI Vision / Gemini multimodal (smoke, crop disease, infrastructure analysis), MediaPipe |
| **Geospatial & Mapping** | Google Maps Platform (hotspot mapping), Earth Engine (satellite imagery) |
| **Data & Backend** | BigQuery, Firebase (auth, real-time DB, hosting), Cloud Run / Cloud Functions |
| **Apps** | Flutter or Android (citizen-facing apps); WhatsApp Business API or basic SMS gateways for low-connectivity access |
| **Public Data** | data.gov.in, Census/NFHS datasets, CPCB air quality data, IMD weather data, state agriculture department datasets |

### Evaluation Parameters

| Criterion | Weight | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| Problem-Solution Fit | 20% | Does it directly address the stated problem, with the right scope? |
| AI/Technical Execution | 25% | Is the AI doing real work (not decorative)? Does the prototype function end-to-end? |
| Deployability & Scalability | 25% | Could this realistically run in a real PHC/constituency/district in weeks, and scale beyond one pilot site? |
| Inclusivity & Accessibility | 15% | Multilingual support, voice/low-literacy access, low-connectivity design |
| Impact Potential | 10% | Scale of benefit — how many citizens, how meaningfully? |
| Presentation & Clarity | 5% | Can a non-technical MP's office understand the value in 5 minutes? |

## FAQs

**Q: Do I need to be a developer to participate?**
A: No — teams benefit from a mix of skills: development, AI/ML, design, and domain/policy understanding. At least one team member should be comfortable building the technical solution.

**Q: Can I participate solo?**
A: You can register individually and we'll help match you into a team, or you can build solo if you're confident covering the full scope — though we'd recommend teaming up.

**Q: Which problem statement should I pick?**
A: Pick the one your team is most excited about and best equipped to build for — judging weights are the same across tracks, so there's no "easier" track, only better fit.

**Q: Do I need real government/health/agriculture data to build this?**
A: No. Use public datasets, sample data, or synthetic data that's realistic. We're evaluating the system's logic and design more than data completeness at this stage — though showing you've thought about real data integration will score well on deployability.

**Q: What happens if my solution is selected for a pilot?**
A: The partnering MP's office and GDG will work with your team to scope a pilot in a real constituency/PHC/district. Specific terms (timeline, support, data access, IP) will be shared with shortlisted teams before pilot commitments are finalized.

**Q: Is there a registration fee?**
A: No — participation is completely free.

**Q: Can teams from outside India participate?**
A: No — the hackathon is open only to participants based in India; applicants outside India are not eligible at this time.

**Q: What languages can our submission/demo be in?**
A: English for documentation and pitch decks is preferred for judging consistency; the product itself should support relevant Indic languages where the problem statement calls for it.

**Q: Will GCP/Google AI credits be provided?**
A: Yes — every shortlisted team receives Google Cloud credits to build, test, and scale their solution.

**Q: Who do we contact with questions?**
A: support@hack2skill.com, or the dedicated community channel (Discord/Slack) provided to registered participants.
