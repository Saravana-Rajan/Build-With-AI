# Sarvik — Per-Screen Guide + MP Pitch Script

How to walk an MP through Sarvik, page by page. For each screen: **what it is**, **what the MP sees**, and **what to say**. Demo in this order.

Live: https://sarvik.web.app · One line that frames everything:
> "We don't help you spend your ₹5 crore. We show you the ₹1,600+ crore already owed to your people — and help you unlock it for ₹0."

---

## 1. Dashboard — the overview (open here)
**What it is:** the MP's Monday-morning home. The big number + department summary.
**MP sees:** ₹ owed hero, department cards, top priorities, "villages that never asked", a chart.
**Say:** "This is your constituency at a glance. Top line — **₹1,600+ crore is owed to Coimbatore's people** under schemes they're already entitled to, but haven't received. Below, it's split by department so you know exactly who to push. Everything else drills down from here."

## 2. Intake — the firehose, made clean
**What it is:** every citizen complaint, any channel (WhatsApp, voice, paper, phone), any language, turned into one clean list.
**MP sees:** real Coimbatore complaints (Siruvani water, Vellalore dump, floods…) in Tamil / Hindi / English, tagged by category + place.
**Say:** "Every complaint that reaches your office — voice note, paper petition, phone call — lands here as clean, structured data. These are **real Coimbatore issues**, in the language people actually used. The AI read and sorted all of them automatically."

## 3. Priorities — signal from noise
**What it is:** duplicates merged into real issues, then ranked; each with a reason.
**MP sees:** "300 raw → 79 issues, duplicates merged", a ranked list with a 0–100 score, and a **"why this matters"** line.
**Say:** "You used to drown in hundreds of complaints. We merge duplicates — 50 people reporting one broken road becomes **one issue, counted 50 times**. Then we rank them. And critically — the AI justifies each one: *'genuine gap — nearest school is 15 km away'* versus *'likely already served — a school operates 4 km away.'* So you act on real need, not noise."

## 4. Constituency X-Ray — the map (hero)
**What it is:** a real Google Map + list of where scheme money is failing to land.
**MP sees:** map colored by gap (red = worst), "₹ owed" hero, biggest-gap list with coverage %, "real vs modelled" tags.
**Say:** "This is the X-ray of your constituency. Every ward and village, colored by how much is still owed. Red means the biggest gap. Click any area — you see exactly which scheme, how many people, and how much money is stuck. Where it says **'Real · Census'**, that's genuine government data."

## 5. Departments — whose job is it
**What it is:** the same money, grouped by the department accountable for it.
**MP sees:** one card per department (Housing ₹633cr, Sanitation ₹307cr, Health ₹249cr…) with the **central schemes highlighted** (PMAY, NHM, Jal Jeevan…).
**Say:** "You think in terms of *who do I pressure*. So we group everything by department. Housing owes the most — ₹633 crore — under PMAY. Health, ₹249 crore under NHM. Each card names the exact central scheme to invoke. This is your meeting agenda with each department."

## 6. Act — turn it into action (two tracks)
**What it is:** the decision screen. Track A = unlock entitlements (₹0). Track B = spend your ₹5 cr.
**MP sees:** Track A — per-department **one-click letter** (preview → send). Track B — the ₹5 cr allocation on needs no scheme covers.
**Say:** "Here's where you act. **Track A** — for everything a scheme already owes, the AI drafts the official letter to that department. One click, preview, send. ₹0 of your fund. **Track B** — only the needs that *no* scheme covers (mostly local roads) come to your ₹5 crore, and the AI picks the mix that helps the most people. You spend your money only where nothing else will."

## 7. Forgotten Villages — the silent ones (mic-drop)
**What it is:** high-need areas that never complain — so they never get attention.
**MP sees:** flagged areas with high need score, **zero petitions**.
**Say:** "Normal systems reward whoever shouts loudest. This one does the opposite. These are areas with the worst indicators that have sent **zero complaints** — too poor or remote to ask. The data speaks for them. **This is how you reach the people no one else does.**"

## 8. Scan Petition — the real front desk
**What it is:** upload a photo of a paper letter / a voice note → AI reads it into a structured record.
**MP sees:** drop a file → "AI is reading…" → the extracted complaint (category, place, scheme, Track A/B).
**Say:** "Most citizens still hand in paper. Watch — [upload a photo of a petition] — the AI reads it, finds the location, the issue, and even which scheme it's owed under. The front desk becomes instant and searchable."

## 9. Ask Sarvik — ask anything
**What it is:** a chatbot over your constituency data.
**MP sees:** a chat box; type a question, get a grounded answer with numbers.
**Say:** "And you can just *ask*. [type: 'Which department owes the most?'] — 'Housing, ₹632 crore.' Ask it about any ward, any scheme, any village. It answers from your real data, in seconds."

3. Forgotten Villages — "And here are the ones who never even asked."
4. Act — "One click: unlock it for ₹0. Spend your ₹5 cr only on what's left."
> Close: "Coimbatore today. Deployable for any of India's 543 constituencies tomorrow."
